The First Storytellers

Long before Nashville was "Music City," the Mississippian people (800 CE - 1600s) built complex chiefdoms and earthen mounds[cite: 1, 2]. They introduced the earliest traditions of music through drumming and chanting[cite: 8].

Stewardship

For these tribes, land was a living system to be respected, not property[cite: 11]. This was reflected in sustainable hunting and rotational agriculture[cite: 15].
